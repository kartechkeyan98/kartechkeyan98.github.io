Mathematics

This whole section deals with mathematics (the adult version of it, PURE MATH). What is discussed here is pure mathematics, and things are discussed from scratch, with all the technical fluff in it.

A Short Intro

Mathematics is a very vast and a highly mature branch of science, to the point that right now, writing anything novel in maths is very rare and most of the times esoteric. Hence, what I will delve into are the very fundamental branches of mathematics which are tight with engineering, and develop those from an axiomatic standpoint.

This statement itself is enough to get me bashed online, but I believe in something called "facts are facts". By fundamental branches of mathematics, I mean those like Linear Algebra, Probability, Optimization and more. Technically, these three themselves drive more than 90% of the mathematics going into engineeering.

Secondly, if asked what does a pure math approach have to do with engineering, I believe that at some point, in one's learning journey, one should become familiar with the internal mechanics of the math that drives your systems. It comes in handy as an analysis tool, and maybe will help you design better systems.

NOTE: If you just plan on reading this through and hoping to understand everything, then please don't waste your time. Mathematics is not learnt by reading, it is learnt by practicing, writing down proofs, and solving problems. You are free to try otherwise, but you have been warned.

Please, at least try to derive the proofs by yourself before you go and click the drop-down menu to have a look at what I have done. Otherwise, this may very well be another one of those things you study for courses the night before and conveniently forget the same day after giving the exam.
